Electronic/Electrical Engineering Softwares

An Electronic Engineering AutoCAD and PCB Design course provides practical skills in designing and fabricating printed circuit boards (PCBs), essential for electronic devices. It covers schematic creation, PCB layout, routing, and simulations using CAD software like Autodesk Eagle or Altium. Students learn to apply design rules, handle through holes and vias, and understand the manufacturing process.

Course Content and Skills:

  • Schematic Capture:Creating electrical diagrams and representing electronic circuits.
  • PCB Layout: Arranging components and routing connections on the PCB.
  • Routing: Connecting components via conductive paths on the PCB.
  • Design Rule Checking (DRC): Ensuring the PCB design adheres to manufacturing guidelines and specifications.
  • Simulations:Using software to simulate circuit behavior and verify design functionality.
  • CAD Software Proficiency:Gaining expertise in software like Autodesk Eagle or Altium.
  • Manufacturing Knowledge:Understanding the PCB manufacturing process and its impact on design choices.
  • Soldering Techniques:Learning how to solder components onto the PCB.
  • Troubleshooting and Debugging:Identifying and resolving issues in PCB designs.

Course Duration:

The students are to practice an hour a day for 2 months for the completion of the course.
